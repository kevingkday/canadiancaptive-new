What is a captive insurance company? expand_more

A captive is a closely-held insurance company whose primary purpose is to insure the risks of its owners or affiliates. It is a sophisticated form of self-insurance that allows organizations to bypass traditional commercial markets.

Why should a Canadian organization consider one? expand_more

Captives provide greater control over claims, direct access to reinsurance markets, and potential tax efficiencies. They are particularly valuable when commercial insurance becomes prohibitively expensive or unavailable for specific risks.

How long does the feasibility process take? expand_more

Typically, a comprehensive study takes 8-12 weeks, depending on the complexity of the organization's historical data and the scope of risks being analyzed.
